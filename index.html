What is the garden?

The Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh (RBGE) is a scientific centre for the study of plants, their diversity and conservation, as well as a popular tourist attraction. Founded in 1670 as a physic garden to grow medicinal plants, today it occupies four sites across Scotland.

Royal Botanic Garden, botanical garden in Edinburgh, internationally famous for its beautiful landscaping. The garden, of 62 acres (25 hectares), includes 35,000 kinds of plants and features special collections of rhododendrons, representatives of the heath family, and many Asiatic genera. The herbarium holds about 1.75 million dried specimens. The garden, founded in 1670, is operated by the government.

The impact of the 2019/20 black summer bushfires on Australia's botanic gardens

Dr Brett Summerell of the Royal Botanic Garden Sydney will highlight the impact of bushfires on the Blue Mountains Botanic Garden, Mount Tomah, one of the gardens of the Royal Botanic Gardens and Domain Trust in Sydney, Australia, and on native plant communities across eastern Australia during the black summer of 2019 - 2020. The talk will highlight impacts on rainforests, the Wollemi Pine population, other rare and threatened species and vast areas of eucalypt forests in eastern Australia, and how those species are (or are not) recovering in the 12 months since the fires. This fundraising event has been organised by the Friends of the Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh.
